## Topic and intent
- Primary query: `how to estimate labor costs in construction`
- Search intent: informational / practical estimating workflow
- Audience: small GC, remodeler, trade contractor, or owner/operator preparing a bid
- Content pillar: construction estimating
- Risk: low as drafted because it explains workflow and formulas without wage benchmarks, legal/tax/labor-burden rules, or project-specific advice. Escalate to medium if adding labor burden percentages, wage guidance, productivity assumptions, or cash-flow advice.

## Current SERP evidence
SerpAPI research on 2026-07-14 showed results from Deltek, Housecall Pro, SmartBarrel, hh2, SubmittalLink, LinkedIn, and Procore. Repeated intent themes: labor hours × hourly rate, crew size, labor burden, tracking actuals, and understanding different labor-cost elements.

People Also Ask themes included:
- How do you estimate labor cost?
- What is the formula for calculating labor cost?
- What does labor percentage mean?

No search volume, CPC, or difficulty metrics were used or fabricated.

## Differentiation
Most SERP results either present a calculator/formula or a broad labor-cost management explainer. Mobi’s article should win by giving a contractor-ready workflow: break work into tasks, estimate crew-hours, apply fully defined rate categories, separate assumptions, check production risk, and compare estimate-to-actual after the job.
